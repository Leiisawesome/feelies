## §0. What this document is

Fourteen research threads, twelve trials, five frozen protocols, three park-rule censuses, two frontier characterizations, zero cards past step 2b — and not one number in the record that cannot be trusted. This document is the distillation: what the program *measured* about successful alpha hunting on this platform, organized as the necessary-condition matrix every future candidate must clear, ordered so the cheapest gate kills first.

The retrospective vantage here is the full program — every session, every ruling, every miss — including the orchestrator's own errors (§5), which the per-cycle retrospectives could not see.

### 1.3 The cost–power–magnitude frontier (the binding physics)

The platform's viability identity, per (symbol, horizon, execution mode):

    κ_req = 1.5 × C_ow,stressed / σ_H        [single-stress anchor, one-way units]

Measured on this universe (8 midcaps, 2025-11 → 2026-04 grid):

- **Cost axis:** honest mechanism-derived κ landed 0.146–0.190 (ceiling 0.30). Passive floors 4.66–6.21 bps one-way (OLN 8.69 — min-commission trap); taker floors 9.12–28.40 bps. H=30 closed universe-wide; H=120 passive open only {APP, RMBS} at optimistic κ; H=300 passive six names; H=900/1800 all eight.
- **Power axis (inverts against horizon):** boundaries/session ≈ 78 @300, 25 @900, 12 @1800. The ≥100 census floor is horizon-invariant, so long horizons are structurally power-starved: no symbol individually clears at 1800 even on 20 sessions; pooling was mandatory and still parked twice.
- **Magnitude axis (the deepest finding):** mechanisms honestly priced at κ ≈ 0.15–0.19 realized RankIC magnitudes of **0.019–0.089** — 2–8× below design. Meanwhile the conjunctive proof bar (|IC| ≥ 0.03 ∧ p ≤ 0.01) binds, at achievable n, at IC ≈ 0.08 (n ≈ 1,050) to ≈ 0.22 (n ≈ 150). The one card whose economics and power both cleared (H8 post-expansion; H10) still sat a factor of 2–4 below the bar its own design implied.
- **Escape routes measured shut:** tranche-2 characterization (pack-13) refuted the higher-σ thesis at its load-bearing assumption — daily rv20 does not transfer to intraday σ_H (overnight-gap variance is excluded from horizon returns while spreads price the same risk into C_ow). 1/15 candidates cleared; H=30 closed for all 15.

### 1.4 What the universe *does* contain (so the record is not misread)

Structure exists: the λ-contrast separated populations (baseline stratum reversion −5.4 bps, t −2.5); the :00/:30 occupancy asymmetry (~2.6×) is real; H=900 drift ran +0.089 with certified conditioning. What does not exist on this grid, at this cost stack and data scale, is a harvestable edge **above the institutional proof bar**. The negatives are measurements, not absences of trying.

## §2. The composite feasibility identity (the matrix core)

A candidate is worth a census — the first non-free gate — **iff all three axes clear simultaneously, with margin, on measured inputs**:

    (E)  ECONOMICS:  κ_frozen × σ_H,measured-median ≥ 1.5 × C_ow,stressed
                     per deployable symbol, κ_frozen ≤ 0.30 ceiling,
                     factors named, ratchet-down-only

    (P)  POWER:      measured-arm conditioned episodes ≥ 100 (census floor)
                     with ≥ 130 design margin; percentile-exemption
                     arm-scoped; unmeasured arms budgeted at ≤ 0.5×

    (M)  MAGNITUDE:  required RankIC at achievable n
                     [ ≈ 2.576 / √(n−3) for the p-bar, floor 0.03 ]
                     ≤ the mechanism class's *plausible realized* IC —
                     calibrated against this program's record
                     (ambient continuation classes realized 0.02–0.09)

Three cycles established that on this universe **no (family × horizon × mode × symbol-set) cell satisfies E ∧ P ∧ M simultaneously**: short horizons fail E, long horizons fail P, and the middle fails M. Any future candidate must show, at design time, which measured change in the world (not in the thresholds) moved one of the three.

## §4. Process factors that made the record trustworthy (keep all of them)

1. **Pre-registration with self-executing consequence matrices.** The evolution from H8's post-outcome precedence conflict to H12/H13's zero-adjudication matrices is the single largest process gain. Freeze the verdict map, not just the thresholds.
2. **Ordering B (evidence before implementation).** H10 died at 2b for the cost of one sensor and three scripts; H8 died at the same gate after eight YAML commits. Phase B gates on step-2 PASS, always.
3. **Measurement over projection — in both directions.** Censuses ran even when deterministically or expectedly parked (H8 primary, H13), and the H13 F2 arm surprised *upward*. Projections never rescue and never condemn.
4. **Cold-read review before every confirmation.** The dossiers overrode the authoring session's recommendation twice on evidence (H1's cost failure; slate-C's strategy override) and concurred once (H12) — and caught the H13 κ product bug and the slate-B short-side inconsistency. Fresh context grading frozen text is not optional ceremony.
5. **Honest-N ledger with drafted-vs-evaluated discipline.** Twelve trials across three cycles, every increment cited. DSR at N=12 stays meaningful; the same program with silent variant-shopping would have N in the hundreds and no trustworthy number anywhere.
6. **One-shot expansions, frozen grids, mechanical draws.** The grid amendment was derived before drawing, drawn by the frozen procedure, and never repeated. Data augmentation under pre-registered rules is legal; data accumulation under outcome pressure is not.
7. **Contamination shelves with firewalls.** Outcome observations (RMBS +0.226, the baseline reversion, the horizon-magnitude series) are labeled, shelved, and citable only as disclosed priors with fresh evidence — never as evidence.
8. **Session hygiene.** Fresh sessions for evidence-class work; state in the repo, not in chat; worktree-clean provenance; PYTHONHASHSEED=0; bit-identical rerun on every artifact.

## §6. What the matrix implies for the next round (mapped to the frozen reopening conditions)

The matrix does not authorize a next round; pack-12's reopening conditions do. What the matrix adds is *which* conditions move which axis:

1. **Cost-frontier structural change → moves E.** A tick-regime reduction on constrained names or a fee-structure change lowers C_ow directly; κ_req falls proportionally. This is the highest-leverage reopening class because E was the binding axis at the density-rich short horizons — the one place P is free. Any such change is measurable with a pack-13-class characterization before a single card is drafted, and any scheduled change date is a pre-registered structural boundary, never a mid-grid surprise.
2. **Data-scale change → moves P and softens M.** A vendor answer extending the admissible span, or a multi-month accumulation program, raises n: the p-bar's required IC falls as 1/√n (n×4 halves it). But the calibration record warns: more sessions at the same geometry do not fix arm-occupancy misses, and the magnitude floor 0.03 is n-invariant by design.
3. **Mechanism-class change → moves M.** The only way to meet the proof bar at this grid's n is a mechanism class whose *plausible realized* IC is 3–10× the ambient-continuation record — i.e., event- or state-concentrated effects where the conditioning captures a genuinely rare, strong episode rather than a persistent weak drift. Such classes are power-expensive by construction (rare episodes), so they collide with P — the E∧P∧M identity must be run per cell, not per axis.
4. **What never reopens anything: threshold movement.** The bars are the product. A "next round" that meets them by softening them has produced numbers this program's record would refuse to sit next to.

Sequencing rule for any reopened round, inherited from what worked: characterization gate (pack-13 class, frozen go/no-go) → slate under this matrix with G-E as a new mandatory card block → cold read → the standard machine. Cycle budget and stop-rule frozen before slate one.
